1. Problem & Opportunity
Problem: Most organizations suffer from "meeting bloat." Meetings are scheduled by default, often lacking clear agendas, running over time, and involving unnecessary attendees. This behavior persists because the cost of a meeting is invisible. An hour-long meeting with 8 people isn't "one hour"; it's an 8-person-hour "cost" pulled directly from the budget, interrupting 8 separate workflows. 
Opportunity: By making the cost of meetings tangible and visible in real-time, you can create powerful behavioral "nudges." Teams and managers can see the financial and time-based impact of their meeting habits, empowering them to be more intentional about when they schedule a meeting versus when they send an async update.

3. Expansion: The "Meeting Pre-Flight Check"
A key area to expand is before the meeting is even scheduled. The app should integrate directly with Google Calendar and Outlook.
When a user creates a new meeting invite:
Attendee Cost: As they add attendees, a small "Estimated Cost for this 1 hour:" counter ticks up in real-time. (e.g., "Est. Cost: $750").
Agenda Nudge: If there is no text in the "Description" (agenda) field, the "Send" button turns yellow, and a warning appears: "Meetings without an agenda are 50% less effective. Add a few bullet points?"
The "Async" Button: A new button appears next to "Send" that says "Request Async Update Instead." This could send a pre-formatted email or Slack message to the group (e.g., "For the topic of 'Project Update,' can everyone please provide a 1-paragraph summary by EOD Tuesday?").

4. Prototype Overview: "The Money Burner 3000"
The prototype would consist of three main parts:
Part 1: The Setup (Admin/User)
A simple web dashboard where:
A team admin sets up the team and invites members.
The admin inputs estimated hourly salary bands (e.g., "Junior: $45/hr," "Senior: $75/hr," "Manager: $100/hr"). This is for calculation and can be anonymized.
Individual users connect their calendars (Google/Outlook).
Part 2: The Live Meeting Plugin (The "Burner")
This is a browser extension or desktop app overlay for Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams.
When a meeting starts, a small, non-intrusive box appears in the corner.
It shows:
Live Cost: A real-time counter: "This meeting has cost: $127.50"
The Animation: A small, pixelated animation of a dollar bill burning.
Timer: A clock showing "Scheduled End: 4:00 PM" vs. "Current Time: 4:05 PM."
The "Yakety Sax" Easter Egg: If the meeting runs 10 minutes (or 2x) over, a small button appears: "Play the 'runover' music?" (It should be user-initiated to avoid total chaos, but the prompt itself is the joke).
Part 3: The Post-Meeting Dashboard (The "ROI Calculator")
After the meeting, the user (or PM) gets a "receipt." This data feeds into a personal dashboard.
The dashboard shows: "This week, you spent 12 hours in meetings, costing an estimated $1,450."
It lists your past meetings with their "Meeting Efficiency Score."
The Score (e.g., "C+ | Could've Been Napping"): The prototype calculates this with a simple algorithm:
Starts with 100 points.
-10 points if it ran over time.
-15 points if it had no agenda.
-20 points if it had >8 attendees (inefficient).
+10 points if it ended early.
+10 points if it was under 30 minutes.

Should we track external meeting costs (clients, vendors) differently?
What's the right frequency for nudges/notifications without becoming annoying?

Privacy concerns: Employees may resist tracking if they feel surveilled; need to position as team-level optimization, not individual monitoring
